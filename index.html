The first frame

MTRA doesn't ask what the ground is. It asks how dangerous it is.

Not a class label — a continuous traversability score in [0, 1], one per pixel. Safe soil sits near the top; a boulder you must not drive over sits near the bottom.

The domain gap

Three missions never designed to be seen by the same model.

Prior work trains on one rover at a time. Built on AI4MARS v0.6, MTRA takes Perseverance alongside Curiosity, Spirit and Opportunity — and the gap between their cameras is measured, not assumed.

Architecture · 100.2M parameters

One shared backbone. Three ways in. A confidence interval on every pixel.

Sensor-specific quirks are absorbed at the edges; terrain semantics are learned once and shared across every mission.

STAGE 1

Mission Adapter

305 params/rover, 915 total. Depthwise-separable conv, zero-init output — identity at epoch 0. Never frozen; co-adapts with the backbone.

STAGE 2

ConvNeXt V2-Base

87.7M params. FCMAE pretrained, then ImageNet-22K/1K fine-tuned. Learns features shared across all four rovers.

STAGE 3

UNet + MC Dropout

Three skip connections. GroupNorm + GRN against feature collapse. Dropout p=0.1 stays active at inference — 30 stochastic passes per image.

STAGE 4

Dual Output

Traversability via sigmoid. The real uncertainty signal is trav_std — the std across the 30 MC passes, not the auxiliary head.

Monte Carlo Dropout

The model doesn't just guess. It tells you how sure it is.

PASS 01 / 30

Every pixel is scored 30 times with different neurons switched off. The average becomes the score; where the 30 answers disagree (cyan), the model is telling you it doesn't know — before it's ever wrong on the ground.
